About

Congratulations on receiving your Software Engineer Interview with Bloomberg!

Here at Bloomberg, engineers have a passion for writing reusable, adequate solutions to complex problems. We keep an open mind and adapt to an ever changing market landscape. Our teams develop software that impacts thousands of users globally.

Take some time today to learn fun facts about Bloomberg and get an idea of how our software engineers define their experiences here. Test your knowledge with our interactive quiz and make sure to provide feedback as Bloomberg is always looking to improve!

A Few Words From Our Engineers

Chris Kulawik

“Bloomberg Engineering is a great community of smart, collaborative, and hard working people. One of my favorite aspects is how well we take advantage of Bloomberg's culture of transparency and eradicate boundaries between teams to get any job done.”

Jacqueline Pan

“Not only do I have an amazing team and management chain that works hard to ensure I'm in an environment where I can thrive, but I also have friends from the other activities I'm involved in around the company such as BWIT (Bloomberg Women in Tech) that encourage and support me.”

Raul Martinez

“One aspect of Bloomberg engineering that has really struck a chord with me, is the freedom to propose a solution, or multiple solution options, when solving a new problem. The exciting thing about having such freedom is that there is rarely one ‘right’ answer and no one will force you to use a technology or approach just because ‘that's the way it's done’.”

Maithu Venkatesh

“Bloomberg has fostered an office environment that helps people working there strike a balance between working hard and being social. I always feel it's possible to meet people for breaks in the pantry. It makes coming to work every day really enjoyable because I know it's very likely I'll bump into a friendly face.”

Theo Rose

“Bloomberg offers an open collaborative environment. Apart from the technology side of things, they provide really good support for individuals through their HR department and Diversity & Inclusion efforts.”

Stefania Samojlik

“I work on a team that builds and supports a graphics library used to create data visualizations. one of the challenges is to build features into the library in a generic way as to be flexible for many different use cases. We approach this problem by researching, thoughtfully designing and reviewing APIs extensively as a team before implementing new features.”
